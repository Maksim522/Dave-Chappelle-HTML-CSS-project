Dave Chappelle

The stand-up comedy legend

Here's a time line of Dave Chappelle's life:

  • 1973 - Born in Washington, D.C.
  • 1990 - Featured in a montage telling a joke in the first episode of ABC's America's Funniest People, moved to New York City to pursue a career as a comedian.
  • 1992 - Won critical and popular acclaim for his television appearance in Russell Simmons' Def Comedy Jam on HBO.
  • 1995 - Made a guest appearance on an episode of ABC's popular sitcom Home Improvement.
  • 1998 - Did a stand-up performance for HBO Comedy Half-Hour, appeared as Tom Hanks' character's friend and confidant in You've Got Mail.
  • 1999 - Appeared in the Martin Lawrence film Blue Streak.
  • 2000 - Recorded his first hour-long HBO special, Dave Chappelle: Killin' Them Softly, in Washington, D.C., starred alongside Norm Macdonald in the comedy film Screwed.
  • 2003 - Debuted his own weekly sketch comedy show on Comedy Central called Chappelle's Show.
  • 2004 - Quit the show walking away from his $50 million contract with Comedy Central.
  • 2006 - In an interview with Oprah Winfrey explained his reasons for quitting Chappelle's Show.
  • 2007 - Set a stand-up endurance record at the Laugh Factory Sunset Strip comedy club.
  • 2009 - Did a four-hour set at Comic Strip Live in New York.
  • 2013 - Returned to full-time touring stand-up.
  • 2014 - Made his first major New York City appearance in eleven years, performing ten nights at Radio City Music Hall.
  • 2015 - Appeared in the Spike Lee film Chi-Raq.
  • 2016 - Made his hosting debut on Saturday Night Live, Netflix announced that they would be releasing three new stand-up comedy specials from Chappelle in 2017.
  • 2017 - Release of 4 Dave Chappelle's specials
  • 2018 - Received a Grammy Award for Best Comedy Album for comedy specials The Age of Spin & Deep in the Heart of Texas, received an Emmy Award for Outstanding Variety Special.
  • 2019 - Was nominated for and won the Grammy Award for Best Comedy Album for specials Equanimity & Bird Revelation, was chosen to receive the annual Mark Twain Prize for American Humor, Chappelle's fifth Netflix special, Dave Chappelle: Sticks & Stones, was released.
  • 2020 - Sticks & Stones won the Grammy Award for Best Comedy Album.
  • 2021 - Starred in his sixth and final Netflix special The Closer.
